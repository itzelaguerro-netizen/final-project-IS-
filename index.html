'Wicked - Part One' Breaking Box Office Records

The Highest Grossing Movie Ever Based on a Broadway Musical


Wicked: Part One follows Elphaba, a brilliant but misunderstood girl born with green skin, as she arrives at Shiz University and unexpectedly becomes friends with the popular and privileged Galinda. Despite their differences, the two bond deeply until a trip to the Emerald City changes everything—Elphaba discovers that the Wizard she admires is responsible for the oppression of talking Animals. When she refuses to support his plans, the Wizard turns the public against her, forcing her to flee and accept the reputation of being “wicked.” Galinda, torn between ambition and loyalty, chooses to stay within the Wizard’s circle, setting the two friends on separate paths as the story ends with their relationship fractured and the world of Oz reshaped by their choices.

Top 5 musical to Movie adaptains with Box office records 2024

  1. Wicked - $634 Million
  2. Mamma Mia - $611 Million
  3. Les Miserables -$442 Million
  4. Grease - $396 Million
  5. Chicago - $306 Million

The film Wicked opened with a historic box-office weekend, earning about $112.5 million in the U.S. and Canada and roughly $164.2 million worldwide in its first days. This debut made it the biggest opening ever for a movie based on a Broadway musical, surpassing previous adaptations and giving it the strongest start of any non-sequel film released in 2024. Industry analysts highlighted how unusual it was for a musical to outperform major franchise films during a holiday release frame.


As its run continued, Wicked surged to more than $700 million globally, combining approximately $460.6 million domestically with $240.4 million internationally. This milestone officially made it the highest-grossing Broadway musical adaptation in film history, overtaking long-standing leaders like Mamma Mia!. After its theatrical wave, the movie continued breaking records by generating $70 million in its first week on digital release in the U.S. and Canada — the biggest first-week digital revenue ever for its studio.


Beyond the numbers, Wicked became a cultural moment celebrated in interviews, cast discussions, and early awards chatter. It was named Best Film of 2024 by the National Board of Review, while director Jon M. Chu received the Best Director honor — marking the first time a fantasy musical had earned that distinction. Across interviews, the cast and creators credited its success to its emotional themes, star power, and its blend of spectacle and nostalgia that drew in audiences well beyond traditional musical-theater fans.
